Oral Communication

Learn how to speak with confidence and connect with your audience

1. Speak Clearly and Confidently

Don’t rush when you’re speaking because people might not understand what you're saying. Take your time, stay calm, and speak at a steady pace. When you speak clearly, people are more likely to listen and stay engaged.

2. Use a Strong, Steady Voice

When you're speaking to a group, it's important to speak loudly and clearly so everyone can hear you. A strong voice makes you sound confident and helps others take you seriously. It also shows that you are sure of what you're saying.

3. Stand or Sit Up Straight

Your posture says a lot about your confidence. Standing or sitting up straight shows you are focused and ready. Great public speakers, like leaders or presidents, use strong posture and presence when speaking.

4. Make Eye Contact

Making eye contact helps you connect with your audience. It shows that you're confident and that you're speaking to everyone, not just one person. Eye contact builds trust and keeps people interested in what you're saying.

5. Practice Before You Speak

If you're getting ready for a speech or presentation, it's important to practice ahead of time. Practicing helps you remember your message and speak more confidently. Try practicing in front of a mirror, with a friend, or record yourself to improve.
